The Pitch – GoodMus

Intro
John describes the tough part of getting ahead as a musician.

The Problem
So much music—so much difficulty finding good music.
Inexpensive recording equipment and synthesized instrumentation have reduced the barrier to entry in the music industry. Anyone can create and publish music these days, and it seems like everyone is, which makes it harder and harder to find good new music outside of traditional channels. There are billions of tracks out there and most of them have never been listened to before by more than just a few people. There’s plenty of good music on the web. How do you find it? How do great artists separate themselves from ordinary artists?
The Solution
Curate all recorded music on the web.
By analyzing, categorizing, and tagging every track on the web through the GoodMus web application, we can easily curate playlists and radio stations from only the good stuff. Finally, music fans will be able to hear talented musicians they’ve never heard before and connect with them before they’ve gone mainstream. This allows artists to sidestep traditional industry methods for content delivery and get on the fast-track to making a living doing what they love.
How
Let users do the heavy lifting.
Pandora has been successful at categorizing music with their Music Genome Project®, which entails musicologists carefully listening to a track, tagging it with a variety of attributes, and adding it to their database. They fall short in two ways:

     1. Only analyzing officially published tracks.
     2. Only allowing their musicologists to analyze tracks.

We intend to not only have qualified musicologists analyze tracks, but also allow users to qualify as a musicologist and analyze tracks themselves. They get to keep their discoveries on their profile and show off their superior tastes, which makes for an engaging and exciting experience.

Once we’ve built a large enough database of tracks we can have our experts create playlists and let our algorithms create radio stations for the rest of the world to consume.

GoodMus is the best place on Earth to find good music by undiscovered artists.
Who
Fans
Our primary target audience are teenagers, young adults, and die-hard enthusiasts of any age. These adventurers love to be the first to find something new. They are already out listening to songs, keeping track of them, and sharing with their friends. But, they don’t have a great place to keep track of their discoveries and show off what they’ve discovered. By creating a fun and exciting experience for them through our platform we’ll keep them interested and coming back for more.

Artists
Artists will benefit from this platform tremendously. I started this project primarily to help them. Any leg up for an artist is huge. Separating the wheat from the chaff will encourage great artists to bring their best music to the world and inspire people others to move. There are plenty of niches for artists that are waiting to be filled and GoodMus is the platform to do it.
The Model
Win-Win
By curating playlists and creating radio stations we are creating value for listeners. Once there are enough channels put together I can start advertising and selling subscriptions to the platform. Artists get paid based on number of plays and we’ll take a percentage off the top. By cutting out the middle-man (record labels, producers, publishers, and distributors), I'll have lower overhead than traditional delivery platforms and can pay artists a larger share than they would normally get.
The Future
Additional channels
Down the road, we intend invite hand-picked artists to play gigs and host concerts in every city and in every neighborhood all around the world. Fans pay for events by donation and support their favorite artists at will. There’s no pressure and no limits. We take a percentage out of every transaction from donations and keep everything from food and beverage sales. The rest goes to the artists.

Let robots do it.
By investing heavily in machine learning and developing innovative sound analysis algorithms, the application can better identify music that is in key, on tempo, and conveys emotion. While this will never compensate for the human ear, it will pre-screen tracks before being analyzed by humans–saving time and resources. In the future, more and more artists will be creating music and the world deserves feel their joy, pain, and love they express—helping us become more connected and in tune with each other.

GoodMus – A better world through music.
